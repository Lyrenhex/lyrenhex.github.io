Hi there!

Hi there, welcome! I'm Damian, a software and website developer from England, so I make cool little services and programs that you can run and use for free. I try to keep things neat and tidy, and I'm a perfectionist, so I aspire for my work to be the best it possibly can be before I will be satisfied with releasing it to the wider public. That said, all my current projects are open-source, free software, for I'm an avid supporter of the Free Software Foundation. (In fact, this very website is open source and licensed under the GNU General Public License version 3. Neat, eh?) I prefer to work in HTML, CSS, JavaScript (mostly Node.JS), and, sometimes, Python.
When it comes to me personally, I love tea - though I don't really think that's much of a surprise now, is it? I'm passionate about my work, and learning, well, anything! From Classical Latin to Pure Mathematics, I enjoy finding out about new things, understanding new concepts, and learning anything at all, really. Of course, I also endeavour to keep up-to-date with specs and standards, especially since I develop websites (like the one you're looking at right now); and the web is notorious for changing standards and introducing new specifications.
I'm also a massive fan of Google, and most things related to them. I know what you're thinking: "But wait, they clash with free software!" Well, yeah, they do - sometimes. But Google are also pretty big supporters of free software (and, in my honest opinion, one of the most transparent companies I know of when it comes to what data they keep), and their Material Design spec is amazing, in my opinion, so...
Otherwise, if I'm not busy working on a project or playing games (because Skyrim is great, heh), then I'll usually be listening to music, hanging out in an IRC chat room... or both. Of course, if you want to chat with me about music or games, or whatever, then please do not hesitate to get in touch! I don't bite, promise!

My Projects

Ave IRC Client

Ave is a new, modern IRC client designed inspired by Google's Material Design style specification. Featuring markdown support and a clean, intuitive, commandless user interface, Ave revolutionises the basic framework offered by the IRC protocol.
Check it out View the source

XeIRC IRC Client

XeIRC is a fairly small project I worked on for a while in Python, which ended up being a retro-styled IRC client. I don't personally still work on it at the moment, but its source is open on GitHub.
Check it out View the source

Ubipy Music Player

Ubipy is a Python / PyGame music player supporting MP3 and Ogg Vorbis files, along with the capability to scrobble to Last.FM and to synchronise your music library with an FTP server.
View the source

Sword Word Processor

Sword is a Visual Basic word processor designed inspired by Google Material Design. Its primary goal was to provide an alternative solution to the dominant Microsoft Word 2013, at the time of its conception.

Free Software

I am, personally, a major supporter of free software, often helping to advocate for its use, and attempting to encourage and congratulate those expanding companies who see the potential in free software and take advantage of the benefits that a free world does afford. (Of course, I'm not talking cost here -- I mean free as in freedom, of safety; libera.)
Consequently, I'm an associate member of the Free Software Foundation, the charity, headed by Honourary Doctor Richard Stallman, which serves to advocate, support, and even sponsor the advancement and adoption of free software.
It's for this reason also that you'll find the majority of my projects to be licensed under the GNU General Public License version 3 -- a free license. In fact, the source code of the very page you're reading right now is licensed under that very one!
I also support Edward Snowden, of whom I have immense sympathy for his unjust situation of being in exile from the very country that he liberated from the secret spying being enacted for years without notice by the US Government. If a country does not trust its citizens, then how can the citizens trust the country?

UK Labour Party

For the past three years I've been a dedicated supporter of the UK Labour party, sticking with them through their difficult turbulence with Mr Milliband, and praising them on their selection of Jeremy Corbyn - promised to be the true embodiment of the Labour beliefs - to be leader of the party.
Having said that, however, the Labour party have been increasingly failing the British public, doing only what is required of them in Parliamentary debates, and not going further than that to fight for what they believe in. Not to say, of course, that I have an issue, per say, with Jeremy Corbyn; I have yet to find reliable evidence to believe him to have failed in his duty, but yet his own party have, for the most part, aligned themselves against him. This, itself, suggests disloyalty within the Labour party - if they can't support their leader, how could they ever hope to support the British public?
Despite, I have stuck with them, enduring the relatively hypocritical outcries from the Conservative party (of whom's leader is the true definition of lightweight). But, on Thursday 17th November 2016, the Labour party have betrayed the people for the last time, when they voted to pass the Investigatory Powers Act, legalising the mass surveillance of every UK citizen indiscriminately and without reason. Along with this autocratic invasion of privacy, the GCHQ have been granted the power to hack into citizen's computers, microphones, and webcams, provided they have simply a warrant. This is not the world I wish to live in where my rights as a human being are cast aside by the very governmental body that promised to protect them.

Liberal Democrats

However, despite my issues with the Labour party, no issue is without its solution. Thus, after desparate searching for an alternative party that actually cares for its supporters' freedoms, I encountered the very party that everyone had forgotten; the solution to our problem, "the only party fighting to keep Britain open, tolerant, and united": the Liberal Democrats.
With Tim Farron, a Member of Parliament who repeatedly demonstrates his care for the working class, even being described as "an ally of farmers" by the NFU, being elected two times thus far as the LibDem leader, it shouldn't be much of a surprise to learn that the Liberal Democrats were the only complete party to vote against the Investigatory Powers Bill in the House of Commons. Not only that, but the Liberal Democrats have also shown consistent support for Edward Snowden, and actively fight to oppose all invasive bills - all while lobbying for a final vote for the British public on the Brexit deal, and whether we consider the terms acceptable or not.
From a political party that was shunned and discarded by the media (unjustly, to my opinion) this goes above and beyond what I would ever have expected. In fact, Lord Paul Strasburger - a strong proponent and Peer of the Liberal Democrats - has oft criticised the totalitarian bills so commonly being pushed by the exponentially far-right Tory government.

I implore you, should you be a British citizen: vote for the Liberal Democrats in 2020. Only then may Britain have a chance at correcting the mistakes of 2016. Is it really so bad to have a party that will fight for you? For your family? For the country?
